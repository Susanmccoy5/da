How To Use A Vehicle Escrow Service To Buy Or Sell A Vehicle
When you hear the word "escrow" you probably think of a mortgage. However, escrow services can be used for the transfer of ownership on many items including jewelry, homes, furniture, and vehicles. When you have escrow, you have an account. This account is not owned by the buyer or seller, but it protects both people involved in any agreement.


Vehicle Escrow Services For Purchase Or Sale
When you are buying or selling a vehicle, you can use a vehicle escrow service. Before the title is transferred, instead of writing a check for the purchase, you put the money in an escrow account. This guarantees your safety since your money is protected until the paperwork is complete.

Once you are sure the title was transferred and you are satisfied, the escrow company releases the money to the seller. This ensures everyone is treated fairly and the seller gets their money. It also guarantees the buyer does not pay for something that they did not receive. If for some reason the deal is not completed, the money is returned to the prospective buyer.


Peace Of Mind for Vehicle Sellers and Buyers
According to the BBB, vehicle escrow services create a "safe environment where both the buyer and seller feel comfortable exchanging money and goods knowing they can't be taken advantage of." When it comes to a large transaction, such as a vehicle purchase, using escrow service is what we recommend. After verifying funds, we alert the Seller that the Buyer completed the payment. Protected from fraudulent checks and money order scams, the Seller has peace of mind knowing funds are behind the eBay shield.


Confidence for Vehicle Buyers
As the buyer, you get the confidence that the vehicle is exactly what you paid for. If, for some reasons, you want to cancel the deal, it will be shiped back to the seller and we will refund your money, straight back to your wallet.


Full Payment Vehicle Escrow Service
We require full payment using vehicle escrow. This might be done to reserve the vehicle for a potential buyer while the vehicle is inspected. If the buyer likes the vehicle, the remainder of the purchase price is added to the escrow account. If the buyer chooses not to buy the vehicle, they get their deposit back.


Vehicle Escrow Service Fees
All vehicle escrow services charge a fee for their services. The fees may be split between the buyer and seller, or paid by one party only. Typically, the parties involved in the purchase decide who will pay the escrow fees before any money is deposited in the escrow account.


How to Use eBay.com for Buying and Selling Used vehicle
Get Complete Confidence When Transacting the Sale or Purchase of a vehicle

Transacting a vehicle sale online involves the exchange of large sums of money between potentially unknown parties. Avoiding fraud is imperative for both buyers and sellers. Fortunately, eBay.com's simple 5-step process ensures money transfer and vehicle delivery with every sale. We will ensure every party receives what was agreed on, every time.

  • Buyer must purchase it - The Buyer initiate a vehicle transaction by completing the purchase online using Buy it Now button
  • Buyer Pays eBay.com - The Buyer submits the payment by approved payment method to our secure eBay Account. Once eBay.com verifies the payment, the Seller is notified that funds have been secured 'In Escrow.'
  • Seller authorization - Upon payment verification, the Seller send us the confirmation to send the vehicle via the agreed shipping method.
  • Buyer Accepts the Vehicle - When the buyer receives the vehicle, they have a number of days equal to the agreed-upon inspection period to inspect the vehicle (5 days). If the vehicle meets the requisite standard, the buyer informs eBay.com they have accepted the vehicle.
  • eBay.com Pays the Seller - eBay.com releases the funds to the Seller from the Escrow Account.
